What are ?

Often times, are compared to the suffixes "Mr." and "Mrs." in English, but the versality, social implications, as well as the grammatical restrictions may have are far more complicated than the gender or marital status of a person. Rather, are chosen based on the degree and nature of respect, or lack thereof, towards a given individual. 


In Japanese, refers to either a prefix/suffix that shows respect to the listener or third-person, standalone words which serve the same function of giving respect to the listener but as pronouns in their own right.
